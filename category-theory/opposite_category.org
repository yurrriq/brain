#+title: opposite category

For a [[id:06fc0ae5-533c-4b99-be3a-26b98dddae39][category]] $\mathcal{C}$, its /*opposite category*/ or /*dual category*/
$\mathcal{C}^{op}$ is formed by reversing the [[id:fca06db8-da2d-434a-888d-16190002dbf1][morphisms]] (preserving
[[id:83c19dfa-4878-456d-be4d-614c5f39a29e][composition]]).

Some interesting properties:

- A [[id:fca06db8-da2d-434a-888d-16190002dbf1][morphism]] $f : x \to y$ in $\mathcal{C}^{op}$ is the same as a [[id:fca06db8-da2d-434a-888d-16190002dbf1][morphism]]
  $f : y \to x$ in $\mathcal{C}$
- $\mathcal{C}^{op}$ has the same ([[id:79449373-1b4e-4539-a9c6-cf60e3706bc5][isomorphic]]) collection of [[id:deb29391-b39d-41f4-aa9b-5840c873ba09][objects]] and
  [[id:fca06db8-da2d-434a-888d-16190002dbf1][morphisms]] as $\mathcal{C}$:
  - $\left(\mathcal{C}^{op}\right)_{obj} \simeq \mathcal{C}_{obj}$
  - $\left(\mathcal{C}^{op}\right)_{mor} \simeq \mathcal{C}_{mor}$
- Reversing the direction of all the [[id:fca06db8-da2d-434a-888d-16190002dbf1][morphisms]] twice results in the original
  [[id:06fc0ae5-533c-4b99-be3a-26b98dddae39][category]]: $\left(\mathcal{C}^{op}\right)^{op} = \mathcal{C}$
