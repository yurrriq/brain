For a [[category]] $\mathcal{C}$, its /*opposite category*/ or /*dual category*/
$\mathcal{C}^{op}$ is formed by reversing the [[morphisms]] (preserving
[[composition]]).

Some interesting properties:

- A [[morphism]] $f : x \to y$ in $\mathcal{C}^{op}$ is the same as a [[morphism]]
  $f : y \to x$ in $\mathcal{C}$
- $\mathcal{C}^{op}$ has the same ([[isomorphic]]) collection of [[objects]] and
  [[morphisms]] as $\mathcal{C}$:
  - $\left(\mathcal{C}^{op}\right)_{obj} \simeq \mathcal{C}_{obj}$
  - $\left(\mathcal{C}^{op}\right)_{mor} \simeq \mathcal{C}_{mor}$
- Reversing the direction of all the [[morphisms]] twice results in the original
  [[category]]: $\left(\mathcal{C}^{op}\right)^{op} = \mathcal{C}$
